Why I Believe Gold Is Headed to Prices Most People Think Are Impossible

I’ve been called crazy for my gold predictions before. In 2002 I told people gold would hit $1,000 an ounce while it was trading at $300. People laughed. They also laughed when I said the global monetary experiment would eventually drive smart capital back toward hard assets that can’t be printed on command.

That disbelief is exactly why moments like this matter. When consensus is still anchored to yesterday’s assumptions, the people paying close attention to the setup can move before the narrative catches up. Gold is not just reacting to fear right now. It is responding to structural demand that is accelerating behind the scenes.

The Setup

Gold climbed 65% in 2025. That kind of move is not a normal market fluctuation. It is a signal. Gold mining stocks did even better, nearly doubling over the same period, while governments and institutions quietly kept building positions. Moves like that tend to happen when a much larger repricing cycle is beginning.

Why Now

Central banks purchased more gold in the last three years than at any point since the U.S. left the gold standard in 1971. China. India. Russia. Turkey. Dozens of smaller nations. All of them are adding to reserves at a pace that tells you the world is preparing for a very different monetary environment.

What most individual investors still miss is that this demand is colliding with a supply chain that has not been built for speed. Traditional miners need time, capital, permits, and luck. When real scarcity meets accelerating demand, price targets that once looked absurd begin to feel conservative.
